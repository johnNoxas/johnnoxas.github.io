What is the UCAT?

The UCAT, or Undergraduate Medicine and Health Sciences Admission Test, is a test taken by students wanting to enter medicine and health sciences undergraduate programs in Australia. The UCAT is used by universities to assist in the selection of students for medicine, dentistry and other health science courses. The UCAT consists of five sections: verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, abstract reasoning, decision making and situational judgement. The UCAT is a computer-based test and is usually taken in late July or early August.
